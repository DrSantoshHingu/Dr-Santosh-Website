Hip replacement surgery, or hip arthroplasty, is a surgical procedure in which an orthopaedic surgeon removes the diseased parts of the hip joint and replaces them with new, artificial parts. These artificial parts mimic the function of the normal hip joint.<\/p>

You may need hip replacement surgery if you have a disease, such as:<\/p>

  • Arthritis<\/strong> (including osteoarthritis and rheumatoid arthritis).<\/li>
  • Osteonecrosis<\/strong> (AVN) :<\/strong> which happens when parts of a bone die due to decreased blood supply.<\/li>
  • NECK OF FEMUR FRACTURE (NOF<\/strong>) : You may also need hip replacement surgery if you fracture (break) your hip from an injury, such as a fall, or from a bone disease.<\/li><\/ul>

    The hip joint is a ball and socket joint and is one of the largest joints in the body. The upper end of the femur (thigh bone) meets the pelvis to create the joint. The \u201cball\u201d at the end of the femur is called the femoral head and fits into the \u201csocket\u201d (the acetabulum) in the pelvis.<\/p>\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t
